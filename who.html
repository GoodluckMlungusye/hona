About us

Health One on One Africa (HONA)

HONA is indigenous Tanzanian non-governmental organization founded in 2023 with its Headquarters located at Mikocheni A along Bagamoyo road Dar-es-Salaam.

Proceed

In the realm of health interventions, a prevalent approach has been the top-down model, the approach which has been adopted by both governments and non-governmental organizations in implementing various intervention packages. While certain positive outcomes have been observed, it is important to acknowledge that one of the major limitations of these interventions lies in their insufficient or complete lack of engagement with end users/beneficiaries. The fact that the intervention implemented does not capture the end users’ requirements compromises their quality, relevance, and long-term viability/sustainability. This situation has not only resulted in the loss of invested resources but also hindered the progress towards attaining several health goals. To respond to these challenges, our organization seeks to introduce innovative problem-solving approaches, methods, and strategies such Human centered health services design.

Health One on One Africa will design and implement Human Centered Tailored Health Solutions. As the name suggests "One on One" signifies the organization's dedication to tailoring and customizing health interventions to meet the unique needs and requirements of end-users. This approach encompasses the design, implementation, and evaluation of health programs, reflecting a commitment to providing personalized and effective solutions. In addition, the use of "One on One" also conveys a sense of accessibility and inclusivity, suggesting that the organization is committed to providing direct and personalized health support to individuals. Recognizing inclusivity as a cornerstone, the organization strives to ensure that health services address the diverse needs of individuals and communities. Furthermore, by recognizing insufficient implementation of research findings HONA is dedicated in bridging the gap between research and practice by ensuring that research translates into practical, impactful solutions for individuals and communities.

At HONA, our core values are the guiding principles that shape our culture, define our identity, and drive our actions. They serve as the foundation upon which we build relationships, make decisions, and pursue our goals. Our core values reflect who we are as an organization, what we stand for, and how we conduct ourselves in every aspect of our operations. With a steadfast commitment to integrity, innovation, and excellence, we strive to uphold these values in everything we do, fostering a culture of collaboration, respect, and continuous improvement. Join us as we embrace our core values and work together to create a positive impact in our community and beyond.

  • Health is the greatest wealth

    We emphasize the intrinsic importance of health as a fundamental aspect of human well-being. Reflecting the belief that good health is invaluable and serves as the foundation for individuals to lead fulfilling lives. By recognizing health as the greatest wealth, we prioritize investments in healthcare, preventive measures, and lifestyle choices that promote overall well-being.

  • The right to health is a basic right and as such is guaranteed by the constitution

    Access to healthcare is a fundamental human right, enshrined in national constitutions and international declarations. It affirms the principle that every individual is entitled to timely, affordable, and quality healthcare services without discrimination. Upholding this right requires legislative and policy measures to ensure equitable access to healthcare for all members of society.

  • Health is everyone’s concern

    We emphasize the collective responsibility of individuals, communities, governments, and organizations in promoting and safeguarding public health. highlighting the interconnectedness of health outcomes and the importance of collaborative efforts to address health challenges. Recognizing health as everyone's concern encourages active participation in health promotion, disease prevention, and advocacy for policies that prioritize public health.

  • Knowing the determinants of health is a precondition for its improvement

    Underscoring the significance of understanding the various factors that influence health outcomes, including social, economic, environmental, and behavioral determinants. By recognizing the complex interplay of these factors, we can develop comprehensive strategies to improve health outcomes and reduce health disparities. Investing in research, data collection, and evidence-based interventions is essential for addressing the root causes of health inequalities and achieving sustainable improvements in population health.

We highlight our strategic priorities, initiatives, and projects aimed at addressing pressing challenges and advancing our vision for a better future. From healthcare innovation and community engagement to advocacy efforts and capacity building, each area of focus reflects our commitment to making a meaningful difference in the lives of individuals and communities we serve. Join us as we explore the diverse facets of our work and discover how we're working towards positive change in our society and beyond.

  • Reproductive Maternal, Newborn, Child, and Adolescent Health + Nutrition (RMNCAH+N)

    Encompasses a continuum of care that addresses the health and well-being of women, children, and adolescents, with a particular emphasis on reproductive health, maternal health, newborn care, child health, and nutrition. By prioritizing RMNCAH+N, we aim to improve access to essential healthcare services, promote healthy behaviors, and reduce morbidity and mortality among vulnerable populations, ultimately contributing to healthier families and communities.

  • Non-communicable diseases (NCDs)

    Non-communicable diseases, such as cardiovascular diseases, cancer, diabetes, and respiratory diseases, pose a significant public health challenge globally. Involves efforts to prevent, diagnose, and manage NCDs through comprehensive strategies that address risk factors, promote healthy lifestyles, and strengthen health systems. By focusing on NCDs, we strive to reduce the burden of preventable diseases, enhance quality of life, and promote health equity for all individuals.

  • Behavior change interventions for health

    Behavior change interventions are crucial for promoting positive health outcomes and preventing disease. Involves implementing evidence-based strategies to encourage individuals and communities to adopt healthy behaviors, such as regular exercise, balanced nutrition, tobacco cessation, and adherence to medical treatments. By promoting behavior change, we empower individuals to take control of their health and well-being, leading to improved health outcomes and reduced disease burden.

  • Health workforce capacity building

    A skilled and motivated health workforce is essential for delivering high-quality healthcare services and achieving positive health outcomes. Involves investing in training, education, and professional development opportunities for healthcare professionals, including doctors, nurses, midwives, and community health workers. By strengthening health workforce capacity, we aim to improve the availability, accessibility, and quality of healthcare services, particularly in underserved areas, and enhance the overall effectiveness of health systems.

Our Purpose

Our Vision

A healthy and thriving Africa, where all individuals have access to high-quality health care services and health education.

Our Mission

To improve health outcomes in Africa through research, innovation, building health work force capacity, and behaviour change for health interventions.

Focus Areas

Reproductive Maternal, Newborn, Child, and Adolescent Health + Nutrition (RMNCAH+N), Non-communicable diseases, Behavior change interventions for health and Health workforce capacity building.

Core Values

Health is the greatest wealth, The right to health is a basic right and as such is guaranteed by the constitution, Health is everyone’s concern, Knowing the determinants of health is a precondition for its improvement.
